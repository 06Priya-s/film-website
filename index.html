A Brief History of Films

The Silent Era (1890s - 1920s)

The early days of cinema were dominated by silent films, with pioneers like Charlie Chaplin and Buster Keaton creating timeless classics. Films were accompanied by live music and had no synchronized dialogue.


The Golden Age of Hollywood (1930s - 1950s)

This era saw the rise of legendary studios like MGM and Warner Bros. It introduced synchronized sound ("talkies"), and stars like Marilyn Monroe, Humphrey Bogart, and Audrey Hepburn became household names.


The Modern Film Industry (1960s - Present)

From the rise of independent cinema to blockbuster franchises like Marvel and Star Wars, modern films have evolved with advanced CGI, diverse storytelling, and global audiences in mind.
